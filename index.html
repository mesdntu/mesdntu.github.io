COMPASS B100 FAQs

What is COMPASS B100 used for?

COMPASS B100 is a tool designed to provide vessel data and evaluate the compatibility of using biofuels. It helps assess whether specific biofuels are suitable for different vessels and offers practical guidance on their application.

Where can I get the login account and password?

The system is currently in beta testing. Please contact us at d-mesd@ntu.edu.sg to obtain a trial account and password.

What is the basis for determining compatibility in COMPASS B100?

The compatibility is determined based on engine parameters and operational parameters.

How does COMPASS B100 help the maritime industry?

It provides detailed biofuel information and compatibility insights, aiding in maritime sustainability.

What kind of vessels can I evaluate using COMPASS B100?

COMPASS B100 can evaluate a wide range of vessels, including passenger ferries, supply vessels, tankers, and tugboats.

What information do I need to provide to check biofuel compatibility?

You need the vessel name or owner name to retrieve relevant compatibility information.

Can COMPASS B100 evaluate multiple vessels at once?

Yes, COMPASS B100 can be used to evaluate multiple vessels by entering the relevant vessel or owner name keywords.

Can I learn about biofuel options through COMPASS B100?

Yes, COMPASS B100 offers comprehensive biofuel information and guides on their usage.

What is the purpose of the core features in COMPASS B100?

The core features allow users to better understand their vessels, drive sustainability, access a comprehensive database, and receive optimized guidance.

How frequently is the biofuel database updated in COMPASS B100?

The database is regularly updated with the latest biofuel information and compatibility data to ensure accuracy.
